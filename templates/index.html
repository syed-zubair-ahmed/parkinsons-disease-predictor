Parkinson's Disease Detection

Parkinson's disease is a progressive nervous system disorder that affects movement. It usually begins gradually, sometimes with a barely noticeable tremor in one hand. Apart from tremors, it can also cause stiffness or a slowing of movement.

Early symptoms include reduced facial expression, diminished arm swing while walking, and slurred or soft speech. These symptoms worsen over time as the disease progresses.

While there is no cure, medications and sometimes surgery can significantly improve symptoms and quality of life.

Recent advancements in machine learning have enabled the analysis of voice data — called vocal biomarkers — to help detect Parkinson’s disease early.

This tool uses 22 features from vocal signal data. Once entered, a trained model analyzes them to predict the likelihood of Parkinson’s disease.

Note: This system is meant for early screening and awareness. It is not a substitute for professional medical diagnosis.
